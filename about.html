Bio

A Different Kind of Law Student

Jay Haws is a JD/MBA candidate at Wake Forest University School of Law, where his studies sit at the intersection of sports, business, and law. He focuses on Sports Law, NIL (Name, Image, and Likeness) regulation, Business Litigation, and Corporate Law—areas he believes will define the next era of American athletics.

Before law school, Jay was a Division III athlete competing in both baseball and football. That experience gave him something most sports lawyers don't have: genuine firsthand knowledge of what student-athletes face. He knows the demands of balancing academics and athletics, the pressures of recruiting, and the confusion that can come with navigating institutional rules as a young athlete.

After his playing career, Jay moved into coaching—serving as a collegiate baseball coach and working in guest services with the Minnesota Twins organization. Both roles deepened his appreciation for how sports organizations operate at every level, from the front office to the field.

Today, Jay brings that experience to his role as an Athletics Compliance extern at Wake Forest Athletics, where he advises student-athletes on NIL compliance, eligibility requirements, and NCAA regulations. It's practical legal work on the cutting edge of college sports policy, and it's exactly where he wants to be.

The dual JD/MBA degree is intentional: Jay believes that the most effective sports lawyers understand not just the law, but the business decisions that drive organizations. His MBA coursework in strategy, finance, and organizational behavior shapes how he thinks about every legal problem.
